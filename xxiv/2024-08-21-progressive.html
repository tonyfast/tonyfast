    some notes on the design: * every numerical column offers a feature space for formal representation * plotting may switch between scatter, bar, line * columns are features that with views that can be toggled from visual, none, nonvisual, css * visualization transforms tables from relative positioning to absolute positioning. * need to be able to visually swat the axes

    visualization is generally not to be tactile, and if it is the controls are defined in feature spaces. they are offered as something to behold, the author displays their power in controlling information 💪 what if we designed or representations for others to explore. what if we transition from fine art to folk art, from MOMA to meow wolf. there is a terrible void i feel observing calder sculptures from a distance. when you see pictures of him, he is joyfully inside his sculptures, we observe the sculpture from a passive viewpoint while inside is active. the goal of assistive visualization to facilitate the data experience as formal representations. the control space changes when working with tables. > Tables are not pictures of data: they are catalogues of data to be perused, parsed, referenced and interrogated. > A well-designed table will enable the information to be read and understood, and will reveal the patterns and correlations inherent in the data. >> https://alistapart.com/article/web-typography-tables/

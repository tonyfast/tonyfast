up cycling notebooks¶

jupyter computational notebook aestethics are the wild west, and our post-post-modern condition won’t allow for consensus or ideal.

for me, jupyter notebooks are interfaces for hypermedia collage. i can author and create complex compositions in a single place that would otherwise take multiple authoring systems, and more time. notebooks free me from the constraints of both writing and programming wherein they cooperate to help me tell otherwise impossible stories.

as an autistic person, i experience the world through my senses and i feel there is an extent to the freedom of computational notebooks. working with pen and paper is a special interest of mine; the texture, feelings, and experience manifest an authenticity that i’ve never attained from my hypermedia creations.*

* i make this statement in the context of working alone. collaborative work in notebooks can be a transformative experience, it can make ~~and break~~ friendships, while folks discuss their experiences from their different positions.

inside and outside notebooks¶

while learning to work with notebooks it is important to position yourself inside and outside of the notebook. inside the notebook you are the writer, outside you are reader. when we move between these positions were learn the value of different compositions.

inside 🙉 what is the language i need to communicate an idea i am trying to communicate¶

our most common experience with notebooks is from the inside when they are activated by a kernel. inside the notebook we are fighting for language as we bring language to code and vice versa. the time we spend inside the notebook writing exhausts us, it is not sustainable. ultimately, tradition suggests, that most notebooks are destined for an eternal🕱dormancy, rarely to be revived.

outside 🙈 what is being communicated to me when i look at my work with fresh 👀?¶

when we look outside the notebook, on [github], [binder], [nbviewer], [jupyter book], we realize the endurance of notebooks and their value to documentation. the reason the notebook is so versatile is that is stored as json at rest, meaning any programming language can use it.

despite some perspectives, notebooks are not toys if you respect them as tools for writing and composition.

since notebooks are literate programs they not only weave to the aforementioned interfaces, but they tangle to code that can be tested with nbval or testbook and imported with importnb.*

* weave and tangle are conventions in literate programming. weave to a display, tangle to code.

exercise: learning to work outside notebooks¶

jupytercon poster collage representing the atlanta jupyter user group

collage is fun, when we approach notebooks as collages it makes them more fun; collage was an important practice in aspects of the bauhaus teachings. for those that question their artistic abilities please don’t fret because i want you to have fun making a mess.

in my introductions to jupyter i like to ask folks to take a notebook of theirs or a notebook that they have read before and make a physical collage. this exercise brings an outside position to the notebook, it brings a tactile understanding to how we work inside the notebook. we learn to experience our work in notebooks in exciting and otherwise impossible formations.

what you’ll need¶

  • something to print out, feel free to make a custom notebook for the collage if you have time and vision.

  • a printer or a place to print stuff

if you don’t have printer use old books, magazines, and textbooks.

  • at least an hour of creative play to work on your collage.

what you’ll want¶

  • ✂ scissors, 🔪 knife, 🥄 spin, or good old fashioned folding and ripping.

  • tape, glue, stapler, something that make your composition endure

  • something to draw or color with like 🖊 pen, marker, or paint

  • if you have kids, or partners around, bring them into the process if helps somehow. _i don’t know i own 20 ponds worth of 🐶 dogs.

conclusion¶

we don’t have to accept a fate for notebooks that they are rubbish and end up in the bin. we can treat them as things that endure like art and literature. the impact of computational notebook on future writerly experiences will be tremendous. in our software spaces we’ve developed pidgin languages that silo separate large communities by programming language. as software works its way into every nook and cranny of our lives, this language will formalize. til then we get along, but the language is a barrier to entry. collage as an interface suspends the primary need for language and allows us to experience computational works with all of our senses.

please have fun creating your hyper and traditional collages. consider sharing them with the friends you feel comfortable with.

after thoughts¶

  • there is no reason this practice can’t apply to programming language or interface.

  • these ideas definitely echo some prior work like Magnetic Poetry Interface for End-User Programming.
